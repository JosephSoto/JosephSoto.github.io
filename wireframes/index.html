Final Web Design Project

Site Portfolio

Description Although this is a portfolio website, I'd like to use this opportunity to display and curate my design process. Process is integral: the act of making, of creating, and experimenting is just as important as the final product. I find it fascinating to explore the minds of others, their process is a blueprint for that. It presents the designer's tendencies, and their habits. This stage in design is free-forming, and just as beautiful as it's outcome.

Interests

  1. -An image heavy/driven site
  2. -Minimum color palette
  3. -Hidden navigation bar/use of hamburger
  4. -Front page displays work/design process immediatly
  5. -Images bring you to profile of individual works.



Pages

  1. Front page: Name, and a display of work
  2. Secondary pages: Work description, more images, detailed shots of process and final work
  3. Tertiary pages: Contact information, links to other sites





      4. Sketches












      What does my work say about me? I used textures a lot. All of them are hand made. Many are stamped with black sumi ink. Others are photographed. I've recently used marbled ink in one of my booklet designs. Its part of the process I really enjoy the most. I avoid the computer as much as possible when working through my design process. Mark making is effortless and unobjectionable. I also have a delicate hand with typograpghy. Much of my work feels poetic, clean, and elegant.

      How can this be translated into my portfolio site?

      1. -Highlight this particular skillset throughout the site
      2. -Show a video of my process
      3. -Have an animated graphic of textures?
      4. -Introduce one piece of work, (captivate the senses)
